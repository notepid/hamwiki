# Hamfests & Conventions

A hamfest is a gathering of amateur radio operators that typically combines a flea market (swap meet) for buying and selling radio equipment with social activities, technical presentations, and licensing examinations. Hamfests range from small local affairs with a few dozen tables in a car park to massive multi-day conventions attracting thousands of attendees from around the world. For many hams, attending hamfests is one of the most enjoyable parts of the hobby.

## What Is a Hamfest?

The word "hamfest" is a portmanteau of "ham" (amateur radio operator) and "fest" (festival or gathering). At its core, a hamfest is a place where hams come together to buy, sell, and trade equipment, meet fellow operators, learn new things, and have a good time. The atmosphere is typically informal and welcoming, and hamfests are an excellent way for newcomers to immerse themselves in the amateur radio community.

## What to Expect

While every hamfest is different, most share some common elements:

### Flea Market / Swap Meet

The flea market is often the main attraction. Vendors and individual sellers set up tables with new and used equipment, components, accessories, tools, and all manner of radio-related items. You will find everything from vintage tube radios and military surplus gear to modern transceivers, antennas, connectors, cables, test equipment, and project parts. Prices range from bargain-basement to premium, and haggling is often expected and encouraged.

For buyers, the hamfest flea market is one of the best places to find deals on used equipment, pick up hard-to-find parts, and discover interesting items you did not know you needed. For sellers, it is a convenient way to find new homes for gear you have outgrown or duplicates you have accumulated.

### Commercial Vendors

Larger hamfests attract commercial exhibitors who display and sell current-model transceivers, antennas, accessories, and related products. Some manufacturers and major dealers use hamfests to introduce new products, offer show-special pricing, and provide hands-on demonstrations. This is a great opportunity to see and try equipment before buying.

### Technical Forums & Presentations

Many hamfests include a programme of talks, workshops, and demonstrations on topics of interest to the amateur community. Subjects might include antenna design, digital modes, software-defined radio, emergency communications, contesting techniques, satellite operation, electronics construction, and much more. Speakers are often experienced practitioners sharing their knowledge, and the quality of presentations at the larger conventions can rival professional conferences.

### License Examinations

Hamfests are a popular venue for amateur radio licence examinations. In the United States, Volunteer Examiner (VE) sessions are commonly held at hamfests, making it convenient for aspiring hams to take their exam while attending the event. Other countries may offer similar exam opportunities at larger gatherings. Check the hamfest schedule in advance if you plan to take an exam.

### Social Activities

Hamfests are fundamentally social events. Meeting friends old and new, sharing stories, comparing projects, and enjoying meals together are central to the experience. Larger conventions may include banquets, award ceremonies, prize drawings, and evening social events.

## Tips for Attending Your First Hamfest

If you have never been to a hamfest, here are some practical suggestions:

- **Arrive early.** The best deals and most interesting items at the flea market tend to go fast, especially at popular events. Many experienced hamfest-goers arrive at the opening or even earlier.
- **Bring cash.** While some vendors accept credit cards, many flea market sellers prefer cash. An ATM may or may not be available on site.
- **Bring a bag or cart.** If you plan to buy, having a bag, backpack, or folding cart makes it much easier to carry your finds.
- **Know what you are looking for.** Having a general idea of what equipment or parts you need will help you shop efficiently, though being open to unexpected discoveries is part of the fun.
- **Test before you buy.** If possible, ask to see equipment powered up before purchasing. Many sellers are happy to demonstrate that their gear works. For items sold "as-is," factor the risk into what you are willing to pay.
- **Talk to people.** Hamfests are a great place to ask questions, seek advice, and make connections. Most hams are friendly and glad to help, especially if you are new to the hobby.
- **Wear comfortable shoes.** You may be walking and standing for hours, often on concrete or uneven ground.

## Major Hamfests and Conventions

### North America

- **Dayton Hamvention** (Xenia, Ohio, USA) — The largest hamfest in the world, attracting over 30,000 attendees annually. Held in May, Hamvention features a massive flea market, hundreds of commercial exhibitors, technical forums, and activities spanning three days. It is a pilgrimage destination for hams worldwide.
- **Orlando HamCation** (Orlando, Florida, USA) — The second-largest US hamfest, held in February. HamCation offers a large flea market, commercial exhibits, forums, and VE exam sessions.
- **Hamcation (Huntsville, Alabama)** — Another major US event with a strong technical programme.
- **Numerous regional and local hamfests** — Hundreds of smaller hamfests are held across the United States and Canada each year. The ARRL maintains a hamfest calendar on its website.

### Europe

- **Ham Radio Friedrichshafen** (Friedrichshafen, Germany) — The largest amateur radio exhibition in Europe, held annually in June at the Messe Friedrichshafen on Lake Constance. The event attracts visitors from across Europe and beyond, with extensive commercial exhibits, a flea market, technical presentations, and meeting opportunities for international amateur radio organizations.
- **National Hamfest** (United Kingdom) — Organized by the [RSGB](/community/rsgb), the National Hamfest is one of the UK's largest amateur radio events, featuring traders, a flea market, and a programme of talks.
- **Various national and regional events** — Most European countries host their own amateur radio gatherings, many of which are organised by the national amateur radio society.

### Asia-Pacific

- **Tokyo Ham Fair** (Tokyo, Japan) — Organized by JARL (Japan Amateur Radio League), this is one of the largest amateur radio events in Asia. The Ham Fair features commercial exhibits from Japanese and international manufacturers, a flea market, technical seminars, and operating demonstrations.
- **WIA events** (Australia) — The Wireless Institute of Australia and its affiliated clubs organise hamfests and field days across Australia.

## Organizing a Hamfest

Local clubs often organize hamfests as fundraising events and community-building activities. Planning a successful hamfest involves securing a venue, arranging tables and spaces for vendors, promoting the event, coordinating volunteers, and managing logistics such as parking, food, and licensing sessions. National societies often provide guidance and promotional support for affiliated clubs planning hamfests.
